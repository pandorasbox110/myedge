Carmelita D. Marasigan currently serves as the coordinator and a teacher for the preschool levels at Bloomfield Academy in Las Piñas City. She graduated from the Philippine Normal University (PNU) with a Bachelor of Science in Education degree. She also took up units in elementary education at the said institution.

She has been an author and a coauthor of the Abiva Preschool Series since 2000. She has also attended several education-related seminars, and is a member of Gabay Guro, an organization that helps in the professional development of teachers through literacy training programs.


Elena B. Morales graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Elementary and Secondary Education degree at PNU. She obtained a certificate in Teaching English to Speakers of Other Languages from the Asian Seminary of Christian Ministries, and a certificate in Language Teaching from PNU.

She taught at Elizabeth Seton School and Merry Treasure School (MTS) in Las Piñas City. At present, she is a voluntary craft teacher and preschool teacher in China. She has also attended many seminars.


Lilybeth B. Roy is currently the coordinator and a teacher for the preschool levels at MTS in Las Piñas City. She completed her Bachelor of Science in Elementary Education degree with specialization in Preschool Education at PNU. She has been a preschool teacher for twenty years.


Dalrose Ann C. Sambo graduated from the University of Negros Occidental–Recoletos in 1984 with a Bachelor of Science in Mass Communications degree. In 1986, she completed units in her Bachelor of Science in Education degree at PNU, earning herself a certificate in Teaching Program.

She passed the Career Service Subprofessional Examination in 1982 and the Licensure Examination for Teachers (LET) in 2000. She taught for the preschool levels at Elizabeth Seton School in 1986 and MTS in 1992. She is currently a preschool teacher and, at the same time, the coordinator of the preschool department at the Cavite School of Life (CSL).


Carolina T. Tirona earned her Bachelor of Arts in Sociology degree from the University of Santo Tomas (UST) in 1979. She took up units in Education in 1981 and obtained a certificate in Preschool Specialization in 1983 from PNU. She also took up units in master’s degree in Psychology, major in Educational Management, at UST.

From being a teacher to a principal sums up Ms. Tirona’s career ladder. She taught all subjects in the preschool and primary levels at Elizabeth Seton School, Las Piñas City, from 1979 to 1992. During her tenure at the said school, she also became a coordinator of two extracurricular organizations and underwent training as head of the grade school department. In 1992, she transferred to MTS, where she taught all subjects in the nursery level. In the years that followed, she had become the guidance and preschool coordinator, and the vice principal of the school. At present, she is the principal of MTS.

Ms. Tirona has been an author and a coauthor of the Abiva Preschool Series. Since 1979, she has been conducting and attending in-service seminars in and out of MTS. She also participates actively in various religious and professional organizations. She was the public relations officer of the Las Piñas Private Schools Association (LAPPRISA) from 2008 to 2010 and is currently the secretary and one of its contributory writers.


Elsie T. Torreno graduated from PNU in 1975 with a degree of Bachelor of Science in Elementary Education. In 1980, she received her Master of Arts in Child Study degree from the same university.

Over the past sixteen years, Ms. Torreno has served as a principal in different educational institutions. In 1994, she was the principal of MTS in Las Piñas City. Her administrative experience was enhanced when she held a similar position at Riyadh International School in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia seven years later. She returned to the Philippines in 2004 and became the principal of CSL. At present, she is the assistant to the directress and the preschool head of Bloomfield Academy.

A devoted educator, Ms. Torreno has conducted seminars for teachers from different educational levels all over the country. She has been an author, a coauthor, the coordinator, and the consultant of the early editions of Abiva Preschool Series. She was also the president of the Bacoor Sports Meet Council, a board member of the Cavite Alliance of Private Schools, a member of the Cavite Educational Media Association of Private School Administrators, and the auditor of LAPPRISA.
