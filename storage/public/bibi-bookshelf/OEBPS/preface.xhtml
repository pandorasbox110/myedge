Ladders to Learning Series-Mathematics Kinder 2 (Second Edition) is designed to help pupils gain mastery of basic mathematical concepts and skills and develop confidence in doing mathematical operations. It also aims to train pupils to begin solving computational problems at an earlier age.

The worktext primarily develops number sense and measuring skills. In unit 1, the pupils will learn about the symbols for the numbers 1 to 100 and their order based on their values, and compare their values in different ways. In unit 2, they will get a grasp of number words one to hundreds as well as other types of numbers such as ordinal numbers and roman numerals. They will also learn to classify numbers as odd or even, and skip count. In unit 3, the pupils will learn how to add numbers using different methods, as well as solve real-life problems using addition. In unit 4, the pupils will subtract numbers using different methods, and apply subtraction in word problems. In unit 5, the pupils will learn about fractions, and how they are associated with parts of a whole or a set. In unit 6, the pupils will identify days and months. In unit 7, the pupils will learn about hours and half hours. In unit 8, the pupils will know about money values and their use in real life. Finally, in unit 9, they will learn about measurement, specifically, how to measure length and height. This unit also presents different shapes.

Each lesson has the following components:

Let Us Learn—introduces the main topic of the lesson through a variety of examples and explanations. Some lessons include preliminary exercises to help the pupils learn the figure, property, or operation being discussed.

Let Us Try—provides exercises that test the pupils’ understanding of concepts and allow them to apply the lesson they learned.

Let Us Practice—ensures the pupils’ mastery of the lesson using more hands-on exercises.

It is hoped that as the pupils climb up the ladders to learning mathematics, they will have the competence in applying their knowledge of numbers and figures in the real world. Thus, pupils will be prepared to excel in a mathematically oriented environment of the twenty-first century.
