Different Kinds of Nouns

Nouns are words used to name people, places, things, animals, ideas, and events - Rome, clothes, peace, success, etc.

Nouns are classified as

  • Common Noun - this is the general term for anything - table, chair, airplane, belief.

  • Proper Noun - this is a specific name of anything - Roger, Philippines, Bible.

Six special classes of nouns:

  • concrete
  • abstract
  • mass
  • count
  • compound
  • collective

Properties of nouns:

  • person
  • number
  • gender
  • case

In a sentence, a noun can be used as any of the following:

Subject - what is being talked about

Maria is happy.

Predicate Nominative - same thing as the subject; it is placed after the verb

The prince became a beggar.

In a sentence, a noun can be used as any of the following:

Direct Object - receiver of the action which/who is not the same as the subject

The chef prepared a banquet.

Indirect Object - tells to/for whom or to/for what something was done

Mother bought Nancy a bag.

In a sentence, a noun can be used as any of the following:

Object of the Preposition - answers the question what/where/whom after the preposition

The bus came from Manila.

Appositive - another name for the same person or thing represented by the subject

Baguio, the summer capital of the Philippines, is our next destination.

In a sentence, a noun can be used as any of the following:

Objective Complement - added to the direct object to complete the meaning of the verb

We made Lea the manager.

Direct Address - word by which a person is addressed; it is not the subject

Kiara, the room has to be cleaned.
