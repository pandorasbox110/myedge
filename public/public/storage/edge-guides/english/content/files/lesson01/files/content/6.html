Different Kinds of Nouns

Nouns made up of two or more words are called compound nouns. They can be written as one word, as hyphenated words, or as separate words.

One word - mailbox, motorcycle, centerpiece

Hyphenated words - mother-in-law, commander-in-chief, dry-cleaning, take-off

Separate words - swimming pool, public speaking, major general

However, there is no definite rule and you may use any one style to form a compound word. If you are not sure which form of compound word should be used, it is best to check it on a dictionary.

A compound noun has a meaning which is different from the words which form the compound noun.
