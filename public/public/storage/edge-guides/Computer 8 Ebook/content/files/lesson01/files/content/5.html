Different Kinds of Nouns

A mass noun names an idea or an object that cannot be counted.

It is always in the singular form but its plural can be formed by adding a unit of measurement also known as counter, followed by of as in a glass of milk.

Counters are words or phrases placed before uncountable nouns to denote measurement or weight.

Mass nouns can be concrete like milk or wool; they can also be abstract like happiness or integrity.

Mass Noun

The following are considered as mass nouns.

Things considered as whole units - furniture, luggage, equipment, machinery

Abstract ideas - patience, freedom, honesty, knowledge

Natural observable occurrences and quantities - darkness, adolescence, heat, sunshine

Liquids and uncountable things - water, coffee, oil, wine

Mass Noun

The following are considered as mass nouns.

Material things with parts too small to be countable - rice, sand, popcorn, sugar, salt

Names of substances and materials - bread, meat, butter, copper, cement, cotton

Manner of being or doing - laughter, enjoyment, relaxation, recreation

Various emotions - love, sympathy joy, jealousy

Mass Noun

The following are considered as mass nouns.

Sciences and studies - physics, phonetics, statistics, linguistics

Intangible things - air, vapor, oxygen, smoke

Names of languages - English, French, Filipino, Japanese

Count Noun

A count noun names a person, place, or thing that can be counted. It generally also has a plural form.

The following are considered as count nouns.

Names of persons, things, animals, plants, insects, and their parts - boy, nose, dog, book, cactus, ant

Names of objects with definite shape or form - ball, car, mountain, umbrella, building

Units of measurement and words of classification - gram, inch, degree, type, part, drop, bit, kind

Names of some abstract words - idea, plan, scheme, help, nuisance, insulation, hindrance, rest
