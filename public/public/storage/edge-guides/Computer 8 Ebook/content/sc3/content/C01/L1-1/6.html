Characteristics of Matter

All matter share these characteristics: they occupy space and have mass and weight.

Matter Occupies Space

When you place a book on a table, the book occupies space on the table. The space that it occupies is called volume. You can determine the volume of the book by multiplying its length (l), width (w), and height (h), or v = l × w × h.

You have to use measuring containers like beakers and graduated cylinders to get the volume of liquids. The readings on the beakers and cylinders show the volume of the liquids.
