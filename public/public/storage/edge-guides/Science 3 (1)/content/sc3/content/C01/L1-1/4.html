States of Matter

Gases

A gas does not have a definite size, shape, and volume. Like liquids, gases take the shapes and sizes of their containers. Gas molecules are scattered distantly from each other. They collide with each other in random directions. Because of this, they move faster and more freely than liquid molecules. This makes gases flow more easily and spread quickly in a space or in a container in all directions.
