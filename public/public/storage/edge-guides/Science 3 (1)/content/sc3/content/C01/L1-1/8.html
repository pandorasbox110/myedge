Characteristics of Matter

Matter Has Mass and Weight

All matter has mass and weight. Mass is the amount of matter in an object. An object that has more mass weighs more. The weight of an object is affected by both its mass and the pull of gravity. Gravity is the pull of a bigger body such as a planet on an object. The units of measurement used to determine the weights of objects and people are pound (lb.) and kilogram (kg).

When an object is on the moon, its weight there is less than its weight on Earth. However, its mass remains the same. For example, if you weigh 25 kilograms on Earth, when you travel to the moon your mass will still be the same but you will weigh much less than 25 kilograms due to the moonâ€™s very weak gravity.

You can get the weight of objects using different measuring tools or devices. To get the weight of people, you use a weighing scale. To get the weight of goods, such as fish, meat, and rice, you use a commercial weighing scale. The units of measurement for these objects are milligram (mg), gram (g), and kilogram.
