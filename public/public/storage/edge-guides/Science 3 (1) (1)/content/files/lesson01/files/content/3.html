Different Kinds of Nouns

A common noun is a general name of a person, place, thing, animal, idea, or an event.

It always begins with a lower case letter unless it is at the beginning of a sentence - women, country, makeup, etc.

A proper noun names a specific person, place, thing, animal, idea, or an event. It always begins with a capital letter - Nicola, Romans, Rome, Philippines, etc.

Some proper nouns are made up of two or more words.

  • a book - The Last Juror

  • a country - United Arab Emirates

  • a landmark - Liwasang Bonifacio

  • an event - EDSA Revolution
