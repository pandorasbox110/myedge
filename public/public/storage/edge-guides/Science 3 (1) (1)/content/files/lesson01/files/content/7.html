Different Kinds of Nouns

A collective noun is a special noun that stands for a group of people, animals, or things considered as one unit. Use a singular verb when referring to collective nouns.

The collections may be of:

  • people (crowd, orchestra)

  • animals or other living things (herd, swarm, pack)

  • places (union, federation)

  • things (bunch, pile)

Many times collective nouns are given in a phrase form:

  • a bunch of grapes

  • a swarm of bees, etc

Collective nouns are generally treated as singular even though they represent groups of people, creatures, or things. Therefore, a singular verb is used when referring to collective nouns.

A congregation of nuns is attending the inauguration.

The audience is giving the speaker a resounding applause.

The board of judges has issued a unanimous decision.

The jury will announce its verdict tomorrow.
