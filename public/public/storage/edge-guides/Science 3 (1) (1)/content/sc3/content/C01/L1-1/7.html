Characteristics of Matter

Matter Occupies Space

Different units are used in measuring the volume of liquids. These include the following:

  1. milliliter (mL) and liter (L) in the metric system
  2. ounce (oz.) and gallon (gal.) in the English system
  3. teaspoon (tsp), tablespoon (tbsp), and cup (c) for measuring liquids commonly used in households

The units of measurement used for the volume of solid objects are:

  1. cubic centimeter (cm3)
  2. cubic meter (m3)
  3. cubic feet (ft3)

An irregularly shaped object, such as a stone, also have volume. However, the length, width, and height of such an object are difficult to determine. To get its volume, you have to carry out the displacement method. This method is used to determine the volume of irregularly shaped objects. The steps in the method are as follows:

  1. Fill a measuring container like a graduated cylinder or a beaker with water. Record the volume of the water as v1.
  2. Put the stone into the water in the graduated cylinder or beaker. Measure the volume of the water again and record this as v2.
